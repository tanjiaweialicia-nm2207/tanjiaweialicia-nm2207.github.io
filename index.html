Sexual Assault in Singapore

Research question: How many cases of sexual assault do we see in Singapore yearly?

Image of a depressed girl after getting sexually assaulted

This is a question that has always been on my mind. Sexual assault is one of those things within our conservative Singaporean society that we are aware of its existence but we don't talk more about. Between 2017 and 2020 alone, 9200 reports of sexual assault were made, including rape, sexual assault by penetration, outrage of modesty, and sexual offences involving children and vulnerable victims. However, only a select few cases are prosecuted every year. The nature of sexual assault makes many victims scared to report the crime that happened to them. Many factors like the fear of victim blaming, the intimate nature of sexual assault and/or familiarity with the perpetrator may inhibit a victim's deicison to report the incident. Here, I explore how many reports of sexual assault have been made in the past few years using available datasets, the nature of these reports, and what can be done in the future to encourage individuals to open up and report their cases so they can seek legal justice against their perpetrators.
